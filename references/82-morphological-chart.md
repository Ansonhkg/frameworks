# Morphological Chart

The Morphological Chart is a systematic way to explore a solution space. Break a problem into independent dimensions, list the options for each dimension, and then combine them to discover new possibilities.

## Why It Exists

Many teams stop after the first few ideas they think of. A morphological chart forces broader exploration so better combinations do not get missed.

## How It Works

| Dimension | Option 1 | Option 2 | Option 3 | Option 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | A1 | A2 | A3 | A4 |
| B | B1 | B2 | B3 | B4 |
| C | C1 | C2 | C3 | C4 |

The value is not in the table itself. It is in combining options across rows and testing which combinations are feasible and useful.

## When to Use It

- When the problem has many variables
- When you need to systematically explore all solution paths
- When brainstorming alone is not enough
- When you want to avoid narrowing too early

## Airbus A380 Case Study

Airbus used morphological analysis when designing the A380 cabin configuration. It explored combinations across seating layout, entertainment, dining, and service options before converging on the final cabin design.

## How to Build One

1. Define the problem dimensions.
2. List all meaningful options for each dimension.
3. Combine options to generate candidate solutions.
4. Evaluate the combinations for feasibility and value.
5. Keep the combinations that best fit the goal.
