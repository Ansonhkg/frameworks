# StoryBrand

StoryBrand is a branding framework built on one idea: customers pay attention when your message feels like a story and the customer is the hero.

## Why Websites Fail

Most websites talk about the company, not the customer.

People do not visit a site to admire your history. They want to know what problem you solve, whether it is worth their time, and what they should do next.

## The SB7 Framework

| Step | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | A character - the customer |
| 2 | A problem |
| 3 | A guide - your brand |
| 4 | A plan |
| 5 | A call to action |
| 6 | Success |
| 7 | Transformation |

## When to Use It

StoryBrand is useful when:

- People do not understand what you sell
- Conversion rates are low
- The brand story is too self-focused
- B2B buyers need a clearer reason to act

It works best when your message needs to say, "You are the hero, and we help you win."

## Pentene Case Study

Pentene shifted from being a functional underwear brand to "Strong Is Beautiful," a more inclusive story for women.

The reframe changed the hero, clarified the problem, and made the brand a guide rather than the main character. The result was stronger sales and a clearer brand identity.

## Building a BrandScript

1. Define the hero and the problem.
2. Define your brand as the guide.
3. Define the plan.
4. Define the call to action and the desired result.
5. Test and iterate with real customer feedback.

## History

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2014 | Donald Miller publishes *Building a StoryBrand* |
| 2015-2019 | Brands such as P&G, Nike, and Airbnb begin applying the framework |
| 2020 | The framework becomes especially useful for smaller businesses during digital acceleration |
| 2021-2023 | It spreads into business schools and formal brand training |
| 2024-present | AI tools begin generating BrandScript drafts faster |
