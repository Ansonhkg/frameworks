# Wardley Mapping

Wardley Mapping helps you see where a business or product sits today and where it should evolve next. It maps user value against the maturity of the components that deliver that value.

## Why Strategy Meetings Go Wrong

Many strategy meetings are just people arguing from different assumptions.

Wardley Mapping makes the structure visible. It asks two things:

- Where are we now?
- Where do we want to go?

## The Two Axes

| Axis | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Vertical | Value chain, from user needs to infrastructure |
| Horizontal | Evolution, from Genesis to Custom to Product to Commodity |

## Value Chain

Typical layers:

- User needs
- UI/UX
- Application logic
- Database
- Infrastructure

## Evolution Stages

| Stage | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Genesis | New and uncertain |
| Custom | Bespoke and specialized |
| Product | Standardized product form |
| Commodity | Common, interchangeable, and mature |

## When to Use It

Wardley Mapping is useful for 12- to 18-month strategic decisions, especially when you need to explain where to invest next.

Use it for:

- Value visualization
- Trend and threat sensing
- Strategic positioning
- Product lifecycle management
- Capability planning
- Ecosystem analysis

## UK Government Digital Service

The UK Government Digital Service used Wardley Mapping to redesign public-sector digital strategy and clarify where value lived in the system.

The map helped them prioritize investment, improve service quality, and reduce wasted effort.

## How to Create One

1. Define the customer value proposition.
2. Break the value chain into components.
3. Place each component on the evolution axis.
4. Identify strategic opportunities.
5. Plan differentiation and collaboration.
