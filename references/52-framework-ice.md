# ICE Scoring Model

ICE is a lightweight prioritization model for growth experiments. It scores ideas by Impact, Confidence, and Ease, then multiplies them to create a simple ranking.

## Why Growth Teams Need It

Without a scoring framework, growth prioritization becomes politics.

ICE gives teams a common language so they can compare ideas without relying only on gut feeling.

## The Formula

ICE = Impact x Confidence x Ease

| Dimension | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Impact | If the experiment works, how much will it move the metric? |
| Confidence | How sure are you that the idea will work? |
| Ease | How much time, effort, and coordination will it take? |

Each dimension is usually scored from 1 to 10, producing a maximum score of 1000.

## Example Scores

| Idea | Impact | Confidence | Ease | ICE Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Improve step 2 of signup | 8 | 9 | 4 | 614 |
| Win-back email for inactive users | 9 | 7 | 4 | 578 |
| Rewrite hero banner copy | 4 | 8 | 7 | 384 |
| Add a free trial | 7 | 6 | 7 | 294 |
| Improve FAQ page | 10 | 4 | 4 | 80 |
| Add Google/Facebook login | 5 | 5 | 5 | 125 |

## When to Use It

ICE works best for experimental growth teams that already have a backlog of ideas and need to choose a practical order of execution.

It is not the right tool for product strategy decisions or for deciding what product to build in the first place.

## GrowthHackers Example

Sean Ellis popularized ICE while helping GrowthHackers prioritize growth work.

The framework helped the team sort ideas, focus on the highest-value experiments, and scale faster.

### Example outcomes

- Signup flow improvements reduced drop-off and increased new users
- Win-back email campaigns reactivated dormant users and increased recurring revenue

## Five Ways ICE Fails

1. It does not work well when the problem is systemic rather than isolated.
2. It ignores timing and market windows.
3. It can over-reward old ideas and under-reward true innovation.
4. It misses synergy between ideas.
5. Teams must agree on what each score means.
6. ICE is a prioritization tool, not a strategy.
