# LTV/CAC Framework

LTV/CAC is a SaaS health metric that compares customer lifetime value to customer acquisition cost. It tells you whether growth is actually profitable.

## Why It Matters

Many SaaS companies grow revenue while losing money.

That usually happens because acquisition costs rise faster than lifetime value. LTV/CAC helps you see that clearly.

## When to Use It

Use it whenever you want to understand whether your growth is healthy:

- SaaS businesses
- Subscription products
- Performance marketing reviews
- Channel optimization
- Board reporting

## HubSpot Case Study

HubSpot used LTV/CAC thinking to improve marketing efficiency and profitability.

The big idea was simple:

- Reduce acquisition cost
- Increase customer lifetime value
- Improve the ratio over time

## How to Diagnose and Improve It

1. Calculate LTV.
   - LTV = ARPU x customer lifespan

2. Calculate CAC.
   - CAC = total sales and marketing cost / new customers

3. Compare the ratio.
   - A healthy target is usually greater than 3:1

4. Improve the system.
   - Increase LTV with upsell and cross-sell
   - Lower CAC by improving channel efficiency
