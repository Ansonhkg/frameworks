# OGSM Strategic Planning

OGSM is a one-page strategy framework with four layers: Objective, Goals, Strategies, and Measures. It helps teams turn direction into execution.

## Why Strategy Meetings Fail

A lot of strategy meetings end with big slogans and no next step.

OGSM exists to close that gap. It helps a team move from "where are we going?" to "what do we do next?"

## The Four Layers

| Layer | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Objective | Where are we going? | Become the most trusted fashion platform for Gen Z |
| Goals | What numbers do we want to hit? | GMV, DAU, conversion rate, repeat purchase rate |
| Strategies | How will we get there? | Partnerships, personalization, private traffic, membership |
| Measures | How will we know we succeeded? | KPI targets and owners |

## Example OGSM

| Objective | Goals | Strategies | Measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Become the most trusted trendy fashion platform for young users | GMV, growth, conversion, repeat purchase | Brand partnerships, content community, personalization, virtual try-on, membership | GMV growth, DAU/MAU, conversion rate, repeat rate |

## When to Use It

Use OGSM when the team needs a strategic plan that can move from vision to weekly work.

It is useful for:

- Annual planning
- Quarterly strategy reviews
- Product direction setting
- Cross-team alignment
- Executive reporting

## P&G Case Study

P&G used OGSM-style planning for years, especially in Japan and other international markets.

The key lesson is not the specific company example. The key lesson is that strategy breaks when the Objective, Goals, Strategies, and Measures do not line up.

### What went wrong in the example

- The objective was clear
- The goals were measurable
- The strategies did not connect strongly enough to the actual customer decision path
- The measures existed, but the logic chain was broken

## How to Run an OGSM Workshop

1. Define the objective.
   - State the strategic direction in one sentence.

2. Set the goals.
   - Translate the objective into numeric targets.

3. Design the strategies.
   - Choose the major paths that could achieve the goals.

4. Define the measures.
   - Assign KPIs and owners.

5. Execute and iterate.
   - Review the OGSM regularly and adjust as reality changes.
