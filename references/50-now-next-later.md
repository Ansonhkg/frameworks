# Now-Next-Later Roadmap

Now-Next-Later is a roadmap format that manages expectations instead of locking dates. It is built for uncertainty.

## Why Date-Based Roadmaps Fail

Traditional roadmaps pretend the future can be predicted precisely. When dates slip, trust erodes and teams start chasing deadlines instead of value.

Now-Next-Later works differently:

- Now = what we are focused on right now
- Next = what is likely to come after that
- Later = ideas worth keeping in view, but not committing to yet

The closer an item is to Now, the more detail it should have. The farther away it is, the less detail and certainty it should claim.

## The Three Buckets

### Now

- Mobile app v2 launch
- Search improvements
- In-app feedback system

### Next

- AI recommendation engine
- Enterprise feature improvements
- Performance optimization work

### Later

- AR/VR exploration
- International expansion
- Next-generation architecture redesign

## When to Use It

Use it when you need to communicate product direction without making promises you cannot keep:

- Investor updates
- Sales alignment
- Leadership reviews
- Resource planning
- Customer success conversations
- Hiring and team storytelling

## Intercom's Change

Intercom became a well-known example of moving away from date-specific roadmaps.

Once the dates were removed, the conversation changed from "why is this late?" to "what matters most right now?"

## Five Keys

1. Now must be current work, not finished work.
2. Next should have a trigger or review window.
3. Later must be cleaned up regularly.
4. Do not use this instead of detailed planning for complex projects.
5. Keep it flexible, but still principled.

## History

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2014 | Janna Bastow introduces the idea |
| 2017 | Intercom publicly moves away from date roadmaps |
| 2018 | The format spreads widely in product teams |
| 2019 | More product tools add roadmap views based on it |
| 2020 | It becomes even more popular as teams need flexibility |
