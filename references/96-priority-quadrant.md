# Priority Quadrant

The Priority Quadrant replaces vague labels like "important" and "urgent" with a more disciplined backlog model. It helps teams separate what should be done now, planned later, cut, or explicitly rejected.

## Why It Matters

When every request is described as urgent, prioritization stops working. The real goal is to compare value with time sensitivity and make the tradeoff visible.

## The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| High Value, High Urgency | Must be handled immediately | Do now |
| High Value, Low Urgency | Important but not time-critical | Plan it |
| Low Value, Low Urgency | Weak candidate | Cut, merge, or downgrade |
| Low Value, High Urgency | Pressure without clear payoff | Reject or reassess carefully |

## When to Use It

- In requirement review meetings
- During roadmap planning
- In daily standups when new requests arrive
- When multiple teams compete for the same resources

## How to Put It Into Practice

1. Agree on value criteria.
   - User reach
   - Business impact
   - Experience improvement
2. Agree on urgency criteria.
   - Regulatory deadline
   - Competitive timing
   - Technical risk
3. Train the team on how to score requests.
4. Keep the matrix visible in your planning tools.
5. Review and update it regularly.

## Example: SaaS Quarterly Prioritization

| Quadrant | Requests | Share | Typical Lead Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Do Now | 12 | 30% | 2 weeks |
| Plan It | 15 | 37.5% | 4-6 weeks |
| Cut | 8 | 20% | N/A |
| Do Not Do | 5 | 12.5% | N/A |

This kind of classification makes it easier to allocate resources and explain tradeoffs.

## Common Pitfalls

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Vague standards | Different people score the same item differently |
| Using the method for everything | Simple tasks do not need a heavy process |
| Never re-evaluating | Priorities change as the business changes |

## Related Tool

The idea comes from the Eisenhower Matrix, which was first popularized as a time-management tool. The same logic also works for product backlogs and roadmap decisions.
