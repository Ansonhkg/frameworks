# Theory of Constraints

Theory of Constraints, or TOC, says that system performance is limited by its weakest link. The practical job is to find the bottleneck, use it well, protect it, improve it, and then repeat.

## Why Local Optimization Fails

Improving every part of a system does not automatically improve total output. If non-bottleneck steps are optimized without regard for the constraint, the result is often more work-in-progress, more waste, and no real gain in throughput.

## The Five Focusing Steps

| Step | What to Do | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Identify | Find the constraint that limits the system | You know where the real bottleneck is |
| Exploit | Get the most out of the constraint without major new investment | Current capacity is used better |
| Subordinate | Make everything else support the constraint | Less excess work and less inventory buildup |
| Elevate | Add capacity or investment if needed | The bottleneck is reduced or removed |
| Re-evaluate | Look for the next constraint | Continuous improvement continues |

## When to Use It

- Supply chain and logistics
- Product development and innovation
- Project management
- Manufacturing and operations
- Sales and marketing
- Organizational change

## Intel Manufacturing Example

Intel used TOC thinking in manufacturing to improve throughput without buying more equipment.

### Problem

- Bottleneck steps created queues
- Equipment utilization looked high, but delivery was still slow
- Work-in-progress kept building up

### Response

- Identify the bottleneck and protect its runtime
- Synchronize non-bottleneck steps with the constraint
- Reduce excess inventory and improve flow

### Outcome

- Throughput improved by 30%+
- Work-in-progress inventory dropped substantially

## Applying TOC in a Product or Engineering Team

1. Find the real bottleneck.
   - Is it review, development, testing, or deployment?
2. Improve the bottleneck first.
   - Add automation, simplify handoffs, or shift work earlier in the process
3. Make other steps support the bottleneck.
   - Do not let upstream teams flood the system with more work than the constraint can absorb
4. Keep improving.
   - Once one bottleneck is fixed, another one will usually appear
5. Build a shared understanding.
   - The team should think in terms of system flow, not local efficiency
