# First Principles

First principles thinking asks: what is the most basic truth here, and how do we rebuild from that instead of copying industry habits?

## Aim for 10x, Not 10%

Most business advice tweaks the existing system:

- Increase sales by 10%
- Improve satisfaction by 5%
- Reduce churn by 3%

First principles is different. It asks what the physical or basic limit really is, then rebuilds from there.

## The Three-Step Process

1. Identify assumptions.
2. Strip the problem down to basic facts.
3. Rebuild from the facts.

## Example: Rockets

Instead of asking how to make rockets 10% cheaper, first principles asks:

- What does a rocket actually cost?
- Which parts are true physical necessities?
- Which parts are just industry habits?

That is the mindset that drove SpaceX to challenge launch costs and reusable rockets.

## First Principles vs Analogy

| Analogy Thinking | First Principles Thinking |
|---|---|
| "How does the industry do it?" | "What is fundamentally true?" |
| "We cannot do that because nobody does." | "Does physics actually forbid it?" |
| "It is expensive, so it must stay expensive." | "What is the minimum possible cost?" |

## When to Use It

Use first principles when you need to:

- Question industry assumptions
- Find a new cost floor
- Make strategic choices
- Rethink pricing
- Redesign an organization or incentive system

## SpaceX Case Study

Elon Musk used first principles to challenge the idea that rockets had to be disposable and expensive.

By breaking costs into raw materials, manufacturing, and reuse, SpaceX found ways to reduce launch costs and make reusable rockets practical.

## Five Keys

1. Start with "Why must it be this way?"
2. Find the physical or basic limit.
3. Separate true facts from assumptions.
4. Distinguish immutable principles from changeable habits.
5. Verify with real numbers, not just theory.
