# AARRR Pirate Metrics

AARRR is Dave McClure's growth funnel model: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, and Referral. It helps teams measure the full user lifecycle instead of staring only at revenue.

## Why Teams Track the Wrong Things

Many teams watch the final output and miss the funnel problems hiding earlier in the journey.

AARRR makes each stage visible so you can fix the real bottleneck.

## The Five Stages

| Stage | Question | Typical Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Acquisition | Where do users come from? | Traffic sources, SEO, ads, social reach |
| Activation | Did users get value quickly? | Onboarding completion, first-use success |
| Retention | Do users come back? | DAU/MAU, repeat visits, churn |
| Revenue | Do users pay? | Conversion rate, ARPU, pricing performance |
| Referral | Do users recommend it? | NPS, invitation rate, word-of-mouth |

## When to Use It

Use AARRR when you want to diagnose growth, not just report numbers.

Good fits:

- Early-stage product validation
- Funnel optimization
- Growth strategy planning
- Business health monitoring
- Marketing effectiveness review
- Fundraising and narrative support

## Airbnb Case Study

Airbnb used funnel analysis to find its growth bottlenecks early on.

The team improved trust, onboarding, photos, retention, and referrals. As a result, repeat usage increased and acquisition cost fell.

### Example improvements

- Better photos and listing flow improved activation
- Trust features and host support improved retention
- Referrals and sharing improved acquisition efficiency

## Five Principles

1. Start with data, not intuition.
2. Focus on lifetime value, not just acquisition.
3. Iterate and test continuously.
4. Make product, marketing, and operations work together.
5. Keep the user at the center of every stage.
