## Introduction

Complex business problems usually push teams into one of two traps: either they treat symptoms and never get to the root, or they speak too generally and never reach the real issue. Both waste time.

The Issue Tree, also called a Logic Tree, is one of McKinsey's core problem-solving tools. It uses the MECE principle - mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive - to break a large problem into clean sub-problems so nothing overlaps and nothing is left out.

That makes it useful far beyond consulting. Product managers, operators, and even students use it for structured analysis.

## MECE Tree Decomposition

Start from one problem and break it into 2-4 child nodes. Then break each child node again until you reach leaf nodes that can be tested directly. It combines deductive reasoning with induction.

## When Should You Use It?

Issue trees are especially useful when the problem is uncertain or highly complex. They help you break a big issue into smaller ones or balance several dimensions at once.

## Five Common Mistakes

**1. The problem is vague from the start**
- If the scope is not clear, the entire tree can drift off course.

**2. The split is not MECE**
- Missing a factor or counting one twice makes the analysis incomplete.

**3. The tree becomes too complex**
- Perfect-looking trees can become too dense to understand or use.

**4. The tree ignores change over time**
- Problems are not static, and a tree that ignores dynamics can be misleading.
