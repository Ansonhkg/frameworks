# CIRCLES Method

CIRCLES is a widely used product design interview framework. It gives candidates a structured way to solve ambiguous product questions and present a clear answer.

## Why It Helps

In product interviews, the prompt is often just: "Design a product."

CIRCLES prevents you from freezing by giving you a sequence of steps you can follow under pressure.

## The Seven Steps

| Step | Meaning |
|---|---|
| C | Clarify the problem statement |
| I | Identify the customer |
| R | Report user needs |
| C | Cut and prioritize |
| L | List solutions |
| E | Evaluate trade-offs |
| S | Summarize |

## When to Use It

CIRCLES is useful for:

- Product design interviews
- Product analysis interviews
- Day-to-day product thinking

It is not the right framework for:

- Technical interviews
- Behavioral interviews

## Example

If the prompt is "Design a smart home system," you might narrow it to "Design a smart safety system for older adults living alone."

Then you would:

1. Clarify the problem
2. Identify the primary and secondary users
3. Report the users' needs
4. Prioritize the most important needs
5. List possible solutions
6. Evaluate trade-offs
7. Summarize the recommendation

## Amazon PM Interview Practice

Learnit.ai used CIRCLES to teach candidates how to answer product design questions, including exercises like designing cloud storage systems and other common Amazon interview prompts.
