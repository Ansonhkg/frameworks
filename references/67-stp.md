# STP Marketing

STP stands for Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning. It is one of the core frameworks for building a market strategy that actually reaches the right people.

## Why It Matters

Many products fail not because they are bad, but because the market positioning is wrong.

STP helps you:

- Split the market into meaningful segments
- Choose which segment is worth serving
- Define how you want to be perceived

## The Three Steps

### Segmentation

Split the market into groups with similar needs.

Common dimensions:

- Geography
- Demographics
- Psychographics
- Behavior

The segment must be measurable, reachable, large enough, and meaningfully different.

### Targeting

Choose the segment or segments you want to serve.

Consider:

- Market size
- Growth potential
- Competitive intensity
- Fit with your resources

### Positioning

Create a clear place in the target customer's mind.

Ask:

- Who are we?
- Who do we serve?
- What unique value do we offer?

## Example Segment Map

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Early adopters | Want the newest tech and will pay more |
| Pragmatists | Care about value and wait for maturity |
| Price-sensitive users | Look for discounts and promotions |

## When to Use It

Use STP when:

- Launching a new product
- Repositioning a brand
- Deciding whether to expand into a new market

## Netflix Case Study

Netflix evolved its STP three times:

| Era | Segmentation | Targeting | Positioning |
|---|---|---|---|
| DVD era | Viewing habits and price sensitivity | Convenience-focused users | Cheaper and easier than stores |
| Streaming era | Devices, network quality, content preferences | Digital-native users | Watch anything anywhere |
| Originals era | Content preferences and viewing time | Global audiences seeking quality | More than a platform - a creator |

## How to Execute STP

1. Gather market data.
2. Choose useful segmentation dimensions.
3. Build segment profiles.
4. Evaluate and choose the best target market.
5. Write a clear positioning statement.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

| Framework | Role |
|---|---|
| SWOT | Strategic diagnosis |
| 4P | Tactical execution |
| PEST | External environment analysis |
| Five Forces | Competitive pressure analysis |

## Common Mistakes

1. Using too many or too few segmentation dimensions.
2. Choosing vague or unmeasurable segments.
3. Positioning too broadly.
4. Failing to update the strategy as the market changes.
5. Letting the strategy stay on paper instead of affecting execution.

## Summary

STP is a market-shaping tool. It tells you where to compete, who to serve, and how to win a place in the customer's mind.
