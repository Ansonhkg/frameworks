# Sprint Framework

Sprint is the short, structured work cycle at the heart of Scrum. It helps teams ship in small increments, learn quickly, and adjust before they waste months in the wrong direction.

## Why It Exists

Waterfall development pushes feedback to the end. By the time users see the product, too much time has already been spent.

Sprint shortens that feedback loop. Every 1-2 weeks, the team can ship something runnable, learn from real users, and correct course sooner.

## The Core Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Product Owner | Defines the product direction and priority |
| Scrum Master | Protects the process and removes blockers |
| Team | Builds the product |
| Stakeholders | Provide input and judge the outcome |

## When to Use It

Sprint is a strong fit when you need regular delivery and frequent feedback:

- Mobile app development
- E-commerce iteration
- Innovation projects with uncertainty
- Technical debt cleanup
- Data-driven product work
- A/B test-driven development

## Spotify and Discover Weekly

Spotify used Sprint-style planning to build Discover Weekly in about eight weeks.

The team moved through research, algorithm development, UI work, and launch preparation in short cycles. The feature became one of Spotify's best-known products.

## Five Principles

1. Timebox every Sprint.
2. Keep one clear objective.
3. Hold a short daily standup.
4. End with a potentially shippable increment.
5. Inspect and adapt at the end of each cycle.
