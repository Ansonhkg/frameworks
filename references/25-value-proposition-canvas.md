# Value Proposition Canvas

The Value Proposition Canvas helps teams understand customer pain points, needs, and expected gains, then design products or services that fit those needs.

It was developed by Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur as an extension of the Business Model Canvas.

## What It Is

The Value Proposition Canvas is a strategy tool for designing, testing, and refining how a product or service creates value for a specific customer segment.

It has two parts:

1. Customer Profile
2. Value Map

## Core Framework

### Customer Profile

- Pains: risks, obstacles, negative outcomes, unmet needs
- Gains: desired gains, unexpected gains, functional gains, social gains
- Customer jobs: functional, emotional, and social jobs

### Value Map

- Pain relievers: remove risks, reduce friction, avoid negative outcomes
- Gain creators: deliver expected gains and create surprise gains
- Products and services: the core features and supporting services you provide

### Fit Indicators

- Product-market fit: 80%
- Value proposition clarity: 70%
- Customer understanding depth: 90%

## How to Use It

1. Define the target customer segment.
2. Fill in the customer profile.
3. Design the value map.
4. Check the fit between the two sides.
5. Iterate based on feedback.

## Core Principles

1. Start with the customer.
2. Validate quickly.
3. Keep iterating.
4. Use data and evidence.
5. Work across teams.

## Benefits

- Higher customer satisfaction
- Lower development risk
- Faster product launch
- Better resource allocation
- Stronger competitive advantage

## Common Mistakes

- Making assumptions instead of validating
- Focusing on the product instead of the customer
- Designing once and never iterating
- Trying to serve everyone
- Ignoring the competition
