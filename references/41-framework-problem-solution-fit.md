# Problem-Solution Fit

Problem-Solution Fit asks a basic but critical question: does your target customer really have the problem you think they have, and does your solution actually solve it?

## Why It Matters

Product teams often move too quickly from idea to build. PSF is the checkpoint that comes before product-market fit.

If the customer does not have the problem, or the solution does not solve it, nothing else matters.

## Three-Stage Validation

| Stage | Question | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Problem validation | Do customers really have this problem? | Confirm the pain is real |
| Solution validation | Does our solution actually solve it? | Confirm the product idea works |
| Willingness to pay | Will customers pay for it? | Confirm the solution is worth buying |

## When to Use It

Problem-Solution Fit is the earliest serious stage of product validation.

Good fits:

- New product ideas
- Product-user matching
- Enterprise market validation
- Expanding from personal to business use
- Iterating on an existing product
- Entering a new market

## Superhuman

Superhuman is a classic PSF case. Rahul Vohra spent years validating the problem and solution before the product was widely launched.

### Case metrics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Paid users | 100+ |
| Waitlist | 5,000 |
| Valuation | $33M in 2019 |

The lesson is not just that the product was successful. The lesson is that the team treated validation as the real work.

## How to Validate PSF

1. Problem validation
   - Talk to users, observe behavior, and confirm the pain is real.

2. Solution validation
   - Test prototypes, MVPs, or mockups to see whether the solution helps.

3. Willingness-to-pay validation
   - Test pricing, preorders, or commitment signals.

4. Iterate continuously
   - Keep refining the product based on what you learn.

## Lean Startup Roots

PSF sits inside the lean startup tradition.

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2003 | Steve Blank formalizes Customer Development |
| 2011 | Eric Ries popularizes Build-Measure-Learn in *The Lean Startup* |
| 2012 | Ash Maurya turns lean thinking into Lean Canvas |
| 2015 | *The Mom Test* reinforces the need to validate real problems |
