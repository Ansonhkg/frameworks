# Business Model Canvas

The Business Model Canvas is a one-page framework by Alexander Osterwalder for describing how a business creates, delivers, and captures value. It replaces long business plans with a clear view of the whole model.

## Why It Matters

Traditional business plans can be long, static, and hard to use in real decisions. The canvas compresses the model onto a single page so teams can see the logic at a glance.

## The Nine Building Blocks

| Block | Key Question |
|---|---|
| Customer Segments | Who are we creating value for? |
| Value Propositions | What value do we deliver? |
| Channels | How do we reach and deliver value to customers? |
| Customer Relationships | What kind of relationship do we build with each segment? |
| Revenue Streams | What will customers pay for, and how? |
| Key Activities | What must we do well to deliver the value proposition? |
| Key Resources | What assets do we need? |
| Key Partnerships | Who helps us deliver the model? |
| Cost Structure | What are the main costs? |

## When to Use It

- To describe and test a startup idea
- To compare alternative business models
- To plan new products or market entries
- To support strategic planning and innovation workshops

## Nespresso Case Study

Nespresso is a classic example of a premium business model built around convenience, quality, and recurring capsule sales.

| Block | Nespresso Example |
|---|---|
| Customer Segments | Premium consumers, offices, hotels, and other B2B buyers |
| Value Propositions | Convenient one-button coffee, consistent quality, and choice of flavors and intensities |
| Channels | Official website, e-commerce, premium retail stores, and partner channels |
| Customer Relationships | Membership perks, personalized recommendations, and community events |
| Revenue Streams | Capsule sales, machine sales, and long-term B2B supply contracts |
| Key Activities | Product development, supply chain management, and marketing |
| Key Resources | Patented extraction technology, premium brand image, and global distribution |
| Key Partnerships | Coffee bean suppliers, logistics partners, and retail partners |
| Cost Structure | R&D, manufacturing, marketing, and channel operations |

## Five Principles That Make the Canvas Useful

1. Start with customer segments, because every other block depends on them.
2. Treat the value proposition as a test of how value is created, delivered, and captured.
3. Check whether key activities and key resources actually support the target segment.
4. Look beyond product sales and consider subscriptions, ads, data, and other revenue streams.
5. Treat every block as a hypothesis that must be validated in the market.

## History

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2004 | Osterwalder introduces the concept in his doctoral work |
| 2008 | *Business Model Generation* publishes the full canvas |
| 2010 | *The Lean Startup* helps popularize the framework globally |
| 2014 | Large companies such as Alibaba begin using it in strategy work |
