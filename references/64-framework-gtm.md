# Go-to-Market Strategy

Go-to-market strategy is the plan for taking a product from idea to market success. It covers customer targeting, positioning, pricing, channels, promotion, sales, delivery, and support.

## Why Good Products Fail on Launch Day

A product can be good and still die if nobody knows how to buy it or why it matters.

GTM is the bridge between a product and the market. It turns product value into customer acquisition and growth.

## The Five-Part Canvas

| Module | Question |
|---|---|
| Target customer | Who exactly are we selling to? |
| Value proposition | Why should they choose us? |
| Channel | How do we reach them? |
| Pricing | How should we charge? |
| Promotion | How do we create demand? |

## When to Use It

Use a GTM canvas for:

- New product launches
- Existing product optimization
- Cross-functional alignment

## Slack Case Study

Slack's early GTM strategy combined product-led growth, viral sharing, pricing clarity, and ecosystem integrations.

Key moves:

- Free trial first
- Teams invited teammates naturally
- Third-party integrations made the product stickier
- Content and SEO helped reach the right audience

## Five Common Mistakes

1. Not defining the target customer clearly.
2. Having a vague value proposition.
3. Choosing the wrong channel.
4. Pricing incorrectly.
5. Undervaluing promotion.

## How to Use It

1. Fill in the target customer.
2. Write the value proposition.
3. Choose the channel.
4. Set the pricing model.
5. Plan the promotion system.

## Summary

GTM is the operating plan that helps a good product become a real business.
