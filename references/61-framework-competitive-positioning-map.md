# Competitive Positioning Map

A competitive positioning map places competitors on a two-dimensional chart so you can see where the market is crowded and where the open space is.

## Why It Matters

Most teams say, "We are different," but they do not show where that difference lives in the customer's mind.

The map makes the market visible. The real work is choosing the axes that matter to your customer.

## Example Map

Using a smartphone market example:

- X-axis: Simple to Complex
- Y-axis: Individual to Team

The map shows how products cluster, where the red ocean is crowded, and where a blue ocean opportunity may still exist.

## When to Use It

Use a positioning map when you need to answer:

- New product positioning
- Competitor analysis
- Market trend evaluation
- Investor storytelling
- Brand positioning
- Product direction changes

## Tesla Case Study

Tesla did not win by trying to be a cheaper car.

It repositioned electric vehicles as premium performance products:

- X-axis: Low price to high price
- Y-axis: Environmental focus to performance

That shift let Tesla compete with luxury brands instead of commodity carmakers.

## Five Keys

1. Choose customer-perceived dimensions, not internal technical dimensions.
2. Treat the customer as the decision-maker.
3. Keep the map dynamic; markets move over time.
4. Be patient. Repositioning takes time.
5. Validate the axes with real customer data.
