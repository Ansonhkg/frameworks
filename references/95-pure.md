# PURE Usability Evaluation

PURE is an expert usability review framework. It evaluates a product across four dimensions without needing to recruit users first, which makes it useful for fast iteration.

## The Four Dimensions

| Letter | Meaning | What to Check |
|---|---|---|
| P | Purposeful | Is the product's purpose clear? |
| U | Usable | Can people complete the task easily? |
| R | Reliable | Does the experience behave consistently and recover well from errors? |
| E | Equitable | Does the experience feel polished, humane, and inclusive? |

## Scoring

PURE is usually scored on a five-point scale.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Severe problem |
| 2 | Major problem |
| 3 | Moderate issue |
| 4 | Minor issue |
| 5 | No notable issue |

## How to Run a PURE Review

1. Define the product scope and user scenario.
2. Assemble a small review team.
3. Have each reviewer score independently.
4. Aggregate the scores and identify the biggest issues.
5. Turn the results into a prioritized fix list.

## Ecommerce Checkout Example

PURE is often useful for checkout flows.

Typical findings:

- The entry point to payment is unclear
- Address selection or coupon steps are cumbersome
- Error recovery is weak after a network failure
- Success feedback after payment is too vague

The goal is to catch obvious usability issues before launch or before the next iteration.

## When PURE Is Useful

- Before a design review
- During a quick competitive review
- Before shipping an iteration
- When there is not enough time for full user research

## What PURE Cannot Replace

PURE is not a substitute for real user research. Expert review can spot obvious problems, but it cannot fully capture actual user behavior, emotional reaction, or social context.

## History

| Period | Development |
|---|---|
| 1994 | Early usability evaluation methods become more formalized |
| 2000s | Google standardizes parts of its UX review process |
| 2015 | PURE-style scorecards are used internally for faster review |
| 2018 | The method becomes more common in product teams |

| Tool | Use |
|---|---|

| Task Completion Analysis | Measure task-level success |
| HEART | Connect usability with broader UX metrics |
