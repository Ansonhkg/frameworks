# Mental Models

Mental models are the simplified internal maps people use to understand how a system works. Good products match those expectations instead of forcing users to relearn everything.

## Why It Matters

A smart designer does not try to teach users a brand-new way to think. A smart designer understands the user's existing model and designs around it.

## What a Mental Model Is

| Part | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Cognitive Framework | How people organize and interpret information |
| Accumulated Experience | The beliefs formed from past interactions |
| Expectations | The predictions people make before they act |

## When to Use It

- Product design
- UX optimization
- Team communication
- Problem solving and root-cause analysis

## Clippy vs Notion

| Product | What Happened |
|---|---|
| Microsoft Clippy | Broke user expectations by interrupting work and over-assuming context |
| Notion | Matched modern workflows with modular, adaptable building blocks |

The difference is not just feature design. It is alignment with the user's mental model.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Oversimplifying reality | Edge cases break the model |
| Ignoring cultural differences | Different users think differently |
| Treating the model as static | Mental models evolve over time |
| Designing for yourself | Your model is not the user's model |
| Failing to validate | Assumptions stay untested |

## Academic Foundation

| Year | Milestone |
