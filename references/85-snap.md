# SNAP Decision Framework

SNAP is a framework for helping people move from hesitation to action. The idea is simple: users are not always refusing change. They may be busy, tired, or unconvinced.

## The Four Dimensions

| Letter | Meaning | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| S | Simple | Make the choice obvious and easy to understand |
| N | Now | Make the next step feel immediate |
| A | Anchor | Use a reference point that helps people compare and trust |
| P | Payoff | Show the reward clearly |

## When to Use SNAP Thinking

- Product decision moments
- B2B sales conversations
- Ecommerce conversion optimization
- User growth and onboarding

## Slack Case Study

Slack turned team chat into an essential work tool by making the product feel simple, immediate, familiar, and obviously useful.

The product did not win only by adding features. It won by reducing friction and making adoption easy for teams.

## How to Apply SNAP to a Product Journey

1. Identify the decision points in the journey.
2. Check each point for simplicity, immediacy, anchoring, and payoff.
3. Remove friction where users stall.
4. Add clearer rewards and references.
5. Measure whether conversion improves.

## Why SNAP Analysis Sometimes Fails

| Problem | Why It Hurts |
|---|---|
| Poor execution | The idea is right, but the product changes are incomplete |
| Weak data | Without a baseline, improvement is hard to prove |
| Shallow user insight | The real reason for hesitation is missed |
| No follow-up | The framework is used once instead of continuously |
