# Three Horizons

Three Horizons is a strategy framework for managing today's core business while building tomorrow's growth and the future's seed businesses.

## Why It Matters

Companies usually over-focus on one horizon:

- Only today's business
- Or only future ideas

Three Horizons asks them to manage all three at once.

## The Three Horizons

| Horizon | Timeframe | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | 1-2 years | Optimize the core business |
| H2 | 3-5 years | Scale emerging growth businesses |
| H3 | 5-10 years | Explore seed businesses and future bets |

## When to Use It

Use it for:

- Portfolio planning
- Innovation investment decisions
- Growth strategy
- Business transformation
- Balancing short-term and long-term work

## Amazon Example

Amazon is a classic Three Horizons company:

- H1: core e-commerce business
- H2: Kindle and Prime
- H3: AWS as a long-term bet that became enormous

The lesson is that long-term growth needs deliberate investment, not just short-term optimization.

## How to Implement It

1. Inventory your current portfolio.
2. Evaluate each horizon.
3. Identify risks and opportunities.
4. Allocate resources intentionally.
5. Create a review cadence.
