# Empathy-Driven Roadmap

An empathy-driven roadmap turns a feature list into a story about user needs and product commitments.

## Why Traditional Roadmaps Fail

Flat roadmaps usually list features without context.

That makes them hard to prioritize and even harder to care about.

## The Three Layers

| Layer | Meaning |
|---|---|
| User stories | The real needs and pain points |
| Themes | Groups of related stories |
| Product commitments | The concrete promises the team will deliver |

## How to Build One

1. Collect user feedback.
2. Translate feedback into user stories.
3. Group the stories into themes.
4. Turn themes into roadmap commitments.

## Atlassian and Jira

Atlassian redesigned Jira's roadmap experience to make the product direction easier to understand and easier to explain. The result was a roadmap that looked more like a user-centered narrative and less like a feature dump.

## Examples

| Traditional roadmap | Empathy-driven version |
|---|---|
| "Q1 search optimization" | "Help users find what they need in under 3 seconds" |
| "Q2 mobile app" | "Let teams collaborate from anywhere" |
| "Q3 AI integration" | "Use smart recommendations to reduce decision time" |

## Where It Helps

- Product planning
- Stakeholder communication
- Prioritization
- User-centered product strategy

## Summary

An empathy-driven roadmap helps teams talk about outcomes, not just features.
