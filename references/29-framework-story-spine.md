# Story Spine

Story Spine is a storytelling framework that helps you describe a product or project as a narrative. It was developed by Ken Adams for Pixar and used in films such as *Finding Nemo* and *Up*.

## Why Feature Lists Do Not Create Empathy

Products are usually remembered for their why, not just their what. Story Spine gives you a simple narrative structure that makes a product or project easier to explain and easier to care about.

## The Six-Sentence Structure

Story Spine is built from six sentences. Each one extends the one before it, and the order matters.

| Story beat | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Once upon a time... | Set the everyday baseline |
| Every day... | Describe the routine |
| But one day... | Introduce the disruption |
| Because of that... | Show the response and action |
| Until finally... | Show the resolution |
| And ever since then... | Show the new normal |

## When to Use It

Use Story Spine for:

- Product positioning and planning
- UX and interaction design
- Marketing copy and sales messaging
- Team alignment and communication
- User research and feedback synthesis
- Product iteration and prioritization

## Finding Nemo Case Study

The story of *Finding Nemo* maps neatly to the framework.

| Beat | Content |
|---|---|
| Once upon a time... | Marlin is a father fish raising Nemo alone after losing his partner |
| Every day... | He overprotects Nemo, which makes Nemo feel trapped and embarrassed |
| But one day... | Nemo swims too far, is captured, and ends up in a dentist's office in Sydney |
| Because of that... | Marlin starts a search and meets Dory along the way |
| Until finally... | Marlin and Dory find Nemo after crossing many dangers |
| And ever since then... | Marlin learns to let go, and Nemo becomes more independent |

## How to Use Story Spine Well

1. Define the core goal.
   - Decide what change you want the story to communicate.

2. Describe the routine.
   - Show the user's ordinary life before anything changes.

3. Introduce the inciting event.
   - Add the problem that breaks the routine.

4. Describe the response.
   - Show what the user tries to do next.

5. Describe the resolution.
   - Make the solution and its effect clear.

6. Show the new normal.
   - Explain how the user's life changes after the resolution.
