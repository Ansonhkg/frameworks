# Pain-Solution Matrix

The Pain-Solution Matrix is a simple 2x2 framework for judging product opportunities. It maps how painful the user's problem is against how well the proposed solution works.

## Why It Matters

The real question is not whether a feature can be built. The real question is whether it solves a real problem well enough to matter.

## Four Types of Opportunities

| | Solution Works Well | Solution Works Poorly |
|---|---|---|
| Strong Pain | Golden Opportunity | Overengineering |
| Weak Pain | Not Worth It | Bad Idea |

## What Each Quadrant Means

| Quadrant | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Golden Opportunity | High pain, strong solution | A service that solves a real, expensive problem well |
| Overengineering | High pain, weak solution | A solution that is too complex, expensive, or hard to use |
| Not Worth It | Weak pain, strong solution | A polished feature for a tiny or weak market |
| Bad Idea | Weak pain, weak solution | A feature nobody needs that also solves poorly |

## How to Tell If the Pain Is Real

- Will users pay for it?
- Do users already have a workaround?
- How often does the problem appear?
- What happens if it is not solved?

## When to Use It

- During product planning
- When prioritizing a roadmap
- When deciding what to build first
- When explaining a product opportunity to the team

## Airbnb Case Study

Airbnb noticed that host photos were often weak, which reduced bookings. The company introduced professional photography as a service.

### Why It Worked

- The pain was real: bad photos hurt bookings
- The solution was effective: better photos increased trust and conversion
- The result was stronger listing performance

## How to Apply the Matrix

1. Score the pain level.
2. Score the solution quality.
3. Place the idea in the correct quadrant.
4. Decide whether to invest, simplify, rethink, or kill it.
5. Re-check the matrix as the market changes.

## Product and Sales Alignment

The same logic can help sales and product teams talk about customer pain and solution fit using the same language.
