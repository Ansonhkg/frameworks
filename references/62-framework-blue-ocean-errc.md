# Blue Ocean Strategy

Blue Ocean Strategy is about making the competition irrelevant. Instead of fighting harder in a crowded market, you create a new space with a different value curve.

## Red Ocean vs Blue Ocean

In a red ocean, competitors fight over the same customers with similar products and price pressure.

In a blue ocean, the market space is new enough that direct competition matters much less.

## The ERRC Matrix

ERRC stands for:

- Eliminate
- Reduce
- Raise
- Create

| Action | Question |
|---|---|
| Eliminate | What should we remove entirely? |
| Reduce | What should we lower below industry norms? |
| Raise | What should we improve above industry norms? |
| Create | What should we add that the industry has never offered? |

## When to Use It

Blue Ocean Strategy is useful when:

- The market is saturated
- Products are becoming too similar
- Price wars are hurting margins
- Growth has stalled
- Customer needs are changing

## Cirque du Soleil

Cirque du Soleil created a new entertainment category by removing expensive animal acts and star-driven circus conventions, while raising artistic quality, storytelling, and premium pricing.

That is a classic blue ocean move.

## How to Run a Workshop

1. Define the problem.
2. Analyze competitors.
3. Apply the ERRC matrix.
4. Draw the strategy canvas.
5. Turn the analysis into an action plan.

## Why It Works

ERRC helps teams break the usual value-versus-cost tradeoff.

By eliminating, reducing, raising, and creating in a disciplined way, a company can build a distinct value proposition instead of competing on the same terms as everyone else.
