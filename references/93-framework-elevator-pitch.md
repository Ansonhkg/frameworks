# Elevator Pitch Template

An elevator pitch is a short, clear explanation of your product, idea, or business. The goal is to make the other person understand the value quickly enough to keep listening.

## Why It Matters

You rarely get a long, uninterrupted chance to explain an idea. A good pitch makes the core value obvious in 30 to 60 seconds.

## The Six-Part Story

| Part | What to Say |
|---|---|
| Target User | Who is it for? |
| Core Pain | What problem do they face? |
| Product | What are you offering? |
| Core Value | How does it help? |
| Vision | What happens next? |
| Call to Action | What should the listener do now? |

## When to Use It

- Investor meetings
- Cross-team communication
- Performance reviews and leadership updates
- PRD and feature proposals
- New hire introductions
- Demo days and pitch events

## How to Refine a Pitch

1. Write the full draft first, then compress it.
2. Read it out loud and time it.
3. Test it with someone who knows nothing about the product.
4. Prepare different versions for different audiences.
5. Practice until the pitch sounds natural.

## Slack Example

Slack's early pitch logic was simple:

- Traditional chat tools were noisy and hard to organize
- Work messages got buried in the stream
- Teams needed a better way to communicate around work

The pitch worked because the problem was clear, the payoff was obvious, and the product fit the audience's daily reality.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Speaking in jargon | The audience stops understanding |
| Listing features only | A pitch is not a product spec |
| Being vague | People do not remember generic claims |
| Using one script for every situation | Different audiences need different emphasis |
