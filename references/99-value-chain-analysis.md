# Value Chain Analysis

Value Chain Analysis breaks a company into the activities that create value. Porter's core insight is that competitive advantage comes from the way activities fit together, not from a single asset.

## Why It Matters

Two companies can look similar from the outside and still have very different economics. The difference often comes from how efficiently and strategically they run the chain of activities.

## The Value Chain

| Primary Activities | Support Activities |
|---|---|
| Inbound Logistics | Firm Infrastructure |
| Operations | Human Resource Management |
| Outbound Logistics | Technology Development |
| Marketing and Sales | Procurement |
| Service | Support functions that enable the chain |

Primary activities create and deliver the product. Support activities make the primary activities work better. Profit comes from the margin left after the full chain is executed.

## When to Use It

- When a company is losing to competitors
- When cost leadership or differentiation needs to be explained
- When deciding what to outsource
- When planning a new product or restructuring the business
- When considering vertical integration

## Apple Case Study

Apple's value chain is powerful because each stage supports the same strategic story.

| Activity | Apple Approach |
|---|---|
| Inbound Logistics | Long-term supplier relationships and tight inventory control |
| Operations | Outsourced manufacturing with strong design control |
| Outbound Logistics | Global retail, authorized resellers, and online channels |
| Marketing and Sales | Premium brand positioning and experiential marketing |
| Service | Global support, Genius Bar, and strong post-sale service |

## How to Run the Analysis

1. List every major activity.
2. Assign cost and value data.
3. Assess where value is created.
4. Identify the critical success factors.
5. Look for synergies between activities.
6. Turn the analysis into strategic decisions.

## Applying It to Software Products

| Traditional Chain | Digital Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Inbound Logistics | Data, content, and API acquisition |
| Operations | Development, testing, and deployment |
| Outbound Logistics | CDN, app stores, SEO, and social distribution |
| Marketing and Sales | Growth loops, content, and subscription design |
| Service | Support, community, bug fixing, and iteration |

## History

| Period | Development |
|---|---|
| 1984 | Porter lays the groundwork in *Competitive Strategy* |
| 1985 | *Competitive Advantage* formally introduces the value chain |
| 1990s | Consulting firms make it a standard diagnostic tool |
| 2000s | Internet companies reshape the chain around digital activity |
| 2010s to present | Platform ecosystems move the model toward networks |
