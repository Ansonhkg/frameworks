# How Now Wow

How Now Wow is a simple idea-prioritization matrix. After brainstorming, it helps a team sort ideas by feasibility and impact so the group can stop arguing and start choosing.

## The Problem It Solves

Brainstorming creates abundance. The hard part is convergence.

Once a team has a board full of sticky notes, the real challenge begins: which ideas are worth moving forward, which need more work, and which should be dropped? How Now Wow gives the team a shared way to answer that question.

The framework is simple: place ideas on a 2x2 matrix and judge them by feasibility and impact.

## The Matrix

| | Low Impact | High Impact |
|---|---|---|
| High Feasibility | Now | Wow |
| Low Feasibility | Not now | How |

### Quadrant meanings

- Now: easy to execute and useful in the short term
- Wow: highly valuable and distinctive, with enough feasibility to pursue
- How: exciting but difficult; worth exploring further
- Not now: low-value ideas that should be parked or dropped

## When to Use It

Use How Now Wow when:

- A brainstorming session has produced too many ideas
- The team needs to prioritize a product roadmap
- You want fast alignment without endless debate

## IDEO x Kaiser Permanente

In 2003, IDEO and Kaiser Permanente used this style of prioritization while working on patient experience design. They collected a large number of ideas, sorted them through the matrix, and focused on the ideas with the best mix of impact and feasibility.

### Case notes

- Ideas collected: 127
- NOW execution rate: 85%
- WOW conversion rate: 12% over three years
- Investment in HOW ideas: $500K

The main lesson was simple: the matrix creates a common language for deciding what deserves action.

## How to Run a Workshop

1. Prepare the ideas.
   - Gather the brainstorm output and display it clearly.

2. Explain the matrix.
   - Make the two dimensions and the quadrant meanings explicit.

3. Let people score independently.
   - Do not debate too early.

4. Discuss the disagreements.
   - Resolve the ideas that land near the borders.

5. Decide the next actions.
   - Commit to the ideas that are now ready, promising, or worth further work.
