# TRIZ Innovation Matrix

TRIZ turns abstract innovation into a systematic engineering process. It is the Theory of Inventive Problem Solving, built from the study of hundreds of thousands of patents and designed to help people solve contradictions in a repeatable way.

## Introduction

TRIZ was developed from patent analysis, not from brainstorming folklore. The core idea is that innovation is not magic; it follows patterns that can be learned, reused, and applied systematically.

## The Three-Layer Framework

| Layer | Purpose | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Problem definition | Identify the contradiction | Technical contradictions and physical contradictions |
| 2. Tool selection | Choose the right path | Separation principles and evolution laws |
| 3. Principle application | Turn the idea into a solution | Inventive principles and standard solutions |

## When to Use It

TRIZ is most useful for hard technical contradictions, such as:

- A phone must be thin and have a large battery
- A car must be safe and fuel efficient
- A product must be simple and feature-rich

When conventional optimization stops working, TRIZ gives you a structured way forward.

## Case Study: Samsung Galaxy Fold

Samsung faced a core contradiction while developing the Galaxy Fold: the screen had to fold and still remain durable.

By applying separation logic and local quality principles, the team arrived at a solution that combined ultra-thin glass with a flexible OLED layer.

## How to Apply TRIZ

1. Identify the contradiction.
   - Is it a technical contradiction or a physical contradiction?

2. Choose the right tool.
   - Technical contradictions use engineering parameters and the contradiction matrix.
   - Physical contradictions use separation principles.

3. Look up candidate solutions.
   - Find the relevant inventive principles and standard answers.

4. Validate and refine.
   - Test the idea in the real product context and iterate.

## Core Findings

From the analysis of 400,000 patents, Altshuller concluded that:

- Inventive solutions follow patterns
- Technical systems evolve in predictable ways
- Innovation problems can be translated into standard forms and solved systematically
