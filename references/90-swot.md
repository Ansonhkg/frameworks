# SWOT Analysis

SWOT is a simple but powerful strategic matrix. It helps teams compare internal strengths and weaknesses with external opportunities and threats.

## Why It Matters

Strategy discussions often drift into optimism or pessimism. SWOT forces a more balanced view of the business and the market.

## The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Strengths | Internal advantages the company already has |
| Weaknesses | Internal limitations the company must address |
| Opportunities | External conditions the company can use |
| Threats | External risks the company must defend against |

## Strategy Combinations

| Combination | Meaning | Example Use |
|---|---|---|
| SO | Use strengths to capture opportunities | Grow aggressively |
| WO | Fix weaknesses to capture opportunities | Transform the business |
| ST | Use strengths to reduce threats | Defend and differentiate |
| WT | Reduce weaknesses and avoid threats | Stay conservative |

## When to Use It

- Before launching a new product
- During annual strategic planning
- When the market becomes more competitive
- Before major investment decisions
- When the company is considering a strategic shift

## Apple Before the iPhone

Apple's pre-iPhone SWOT shows how the company used internal strengths to seize a new market opportunity.

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| Strong brand | Limited phone experience |
| Strong design capability | Premium pricing |
| Healthy cash flow | Weak direct channel coverage |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---|---|
| Fast-growing smartphone demand | Strong incumbents such as Nokia and Motorola |
| Demand for better user experience | Rapid technology change |
| Convergence of communication and computing | Patent and platform risk |

## How to Make SWOT Useful

1. Define the exact purpose and scope.
2. Gather enough internal and external data.
3. Keep internal and external factors separate.
4. Stay objective.
5. Turn the matrix into action with SO, WO, ST, and WT strategies.
6. Update the analysis regularly.
