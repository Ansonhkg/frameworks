# Google Design Sprint

Google Design Sprint is a five-day process for solving big product problems quickly. Each day has a clear goal: map the problem, sketch ideas, decide on the best path, prototype, and test with real users.

## Five Days, Not Five Months

The point of a Design Sprint is to put a prototype in front of users fast enough to learn before the team sinks too much time into the wrong thing.

It is a complete iteration compressed into one week.

## The Five Days

| Day | Goal | Main Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Map | Define the problem, set the target, interview a real user |
| Day 2 | Sketch | Share reference ideas, sketch multiple solutions independently |
| Day 3 | Decide | Review ideas, vote, and storyboard the chosen path |
| Day 4 | Prototype | Build a high-fidelity prototype without code |
| Day 5 | Test | Interview real users and decide what to do next |

## When to Use It

Design Sprint is useful when:

- You need a fast test of a new product or feature
- The team is stuck in creative deadlock
- Different departments need shared alignment
- You are redesigning an internal tool or process

It is not ideal when:

- The team is too small to commit five focused days
- The product direction is already validated and only needs small tweaks
- You should be testing market demand with an MVP first

## Slack Case Study

Slack used a Design Sprint to explore enterprise features for decision makers.

The team built a prototype of an admin-style dashboard, tested it with real enterprise users, and learned what mattered most: visibility into usage and ROI.

### Outcome

- The feature direction became clearer
- Users asked for practical analytics and comparison tools
- The idea moved toward a shippable enterprise product

## Five Reminders

1. The Decider must be present.
2. Keep the team isolated during sketching.
3. Make the prototype feel real.
4. Do not explain away the test.
5. Decide whether to continue or stop when the sprint ends.

## History

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2010 | Jake Knapp starts exploring faster prototyping inside Google |
| 2012 | Google Ventures begins using the method with portfolio companies |
| 2015 | *Sprint* is published and spreads the framework globally |
| 2016 | Adobe reports major savings from using the method |
| 2017 | Design Sprint becomes part of mainstream product education |

- Google Docs for sprint notes
- Miro or Mural for collaboration
- Slack and Zoom for remote teams
