# SCAMPER Methodology

SCAMPER is a structured creativity framework built around seven prompts: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, and Reverse. It helps teams generate new ideas from existing products, services, and processes.

## Innovation Does Not Start From Zero

Most breakthroughs come from recombining what already exists, not inventing from nothing. SCAMPER gives you a practical way to explore that space.

SCAMPER stands for:

- Substitute
- Combine
- Adapt
- Modify
- Put to other uses
- Eliminate
- Reverse

Each letter points to a different direction for creative inquiry.

## The Seven Prompts

| Letter | Strategy | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| S | Substitute | What can we replace? | Use plastic instead of metal to reduce weight |
| C | Combine | What can we merge? | Phone + camera = smartphone |
| A | Adapt | What can we adjust for a new context? | Optimize the interface for user habits |
| M | Modify | What can we change in size, shape, or color? | A mini version for portability |
| P | Put to other uses | Where else could it work? | Baking soda for cleaning and cooking |
| E | Eliminate | What can we remove? | A buttonless design that feels cleaner |
| R | Reverse | What happens if we flip the order? | Try pay-after-use instead of prepay |

## When to Use It

SCAMPER works well for:

- Product innovation and improvement
- Problem solving and optimization
- Brainstorming sessions
- Creative writing and design
- Business model innovation

## Apple iPod Case Study

In 2001, Apple used a form of SCAMPER thinking to reshape the MP3 player market.

- Substitute: used a hard drive instead of flash memory, which was cheaper at the time
- Combine: merged a music player with a stylish accessory
- Adapt: optimized iTunes syncing for Mac users
- Modify: increased storage to 5GB, enough for about 1,000 songs
- Put to other uses: the device became more than a player; it became a status symbol
- Eliminate: removed complicated buttons and replaced them with a click wheel
- Reverse: changed the model from buy-then-use to try-then-buy

The result was not just a better player. It helped reshape the music industry and paved the way for the iPhone and the App Store.

## Five Keys to Make It Work

1. Stay open-minded and avoid dismissing ideas too early.
2. Combine SCAMPER with other tools such as brainstorming and mind maps.
3. Timebox each letter so the team thinks quickly.
4. Record every idea, including the strange ones.
5. Test promising ideas with prototypes or experiments.
